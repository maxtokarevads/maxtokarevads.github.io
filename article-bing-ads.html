Why Bing Ads in 2026 Are Underrated (Especially for These 3 Industries)

Max Tokarev 📅 February 2026 ⏱ 6 min read
Quick Answer

Bing Ads (Microsoft Advertising) deliver 30–40% lower CPCs than Google for identical keywords, with strongest performance in legal, financial services and B2B software targeting 35+ professionals. Add Bing when your Google spend exceeds $1,000/month — setup via Google Ads import takes 20 minutes. Budget 15–20% of your Google spend to start.

Almost every PPC advertiser ignores Bing Ads (now Microsoft Advertising). That's exactly why it works — less competition means lower CPCs. I manage Bing Ads as part of my multi-platform PPC services, and the audience skews toward older, higher-income professionals who actually have purchase authority.

Across my accounts, Bing typically delivers 30–40% lower CPC than Google for the same keywords, with comparable or sometimes better conversion rates than Google Ads for specific niches.

Who Actually Uses Bing in 2026

Bing's user base has a distinct demographic profile that matters for ad targeting:

  • Age 35–65 — older professionals and retirees who use Windows defaults
  • Higher household income — Bing users earn 35% more on average (Microsoft Advertising Audience Insights) than the general internet population
  • Corporate Windows users — IT departments often set Bing as default in enterprise environments. This means you're reaching decision-makers at their work computers.
  • US, UK, Canada, Australia — Bing's strongest markets. Minimal presence in Eastern Europe or Asia.

3 Industries Where Bing Consistently Outperforms Google

1. Legal Services
"Personal injury lawyer near me," "divorce attorney," "estate planning" — these keywords cost $40–$80 CPC on Google. On Bing, often $18–$35 for the same terms. The 45+ demographic that needs legal services is exactly Bing's audience. I've seen legal clients get 50% lower cost per consultation from Bing than Google with nearly identical conversion rates.

2. Financial Services & Insurance
Mortgage brokers, insurance agents, wealth managers — also strong on LinkedIn Ads — same story. Older, higher-income Bing users are in-market for financial products. CPC is 30–40% lower, and the audience has higher purchase authority.

3. B2B Software (SMB segment)
Small business owners searching for accounting software, HR tools, or project management — a large chunk of them use Windows with default Bing. Enterprise buyers search on corporate laptops (often Windows/Bing). This is a genuinely undervalued B2B channel.

How to Start: Import From Google Ads in 20 Minutes

The fastest way to launch on Bing is to import your existing Google Ads campaigns directly:

  1. Go to Microsoft Advertising → Tools → Import → Import from Google Ads
  2. Connect your Google account and select the campaigns to import
  3. Review and adjust — Bing doesn't support all Google Ads features, so some assets will be flagged
  4. Set budgets at 15–20% of your Google budget to start (Bing volume is lower)
  5. Enable auto-import to keep campaigns in sync as you update Google

Important: After importing, reduce all bids by 20–30%. Bing's auction is less competitive, so your Google bids will overpay. Let the data settle for 2 weeks before optimizing further.

What Doesn't Work on Bing

  • Fashion, beauty, lifestyle (try Snapchat or TikTok for those) — younger audiences who don't use Bing
  • Mobile-first products — Bing is desktop-dominant; most Bing searches happen on PC
  • Very small local budgets — volume is too low to generate data in niche geos
  • TikTok-style or Gen Z products — wrong audience entirely

Frequently Asked Questions

How much traffic will I get from Bing vs Google?
Typically 10–20% of your Google volume in the US, slightly more in the UK. Lower volume but cheaper clicks. For most accounts, Bing adds 5–15% more conversions on top of Google at significantly lower cost.

Does Bing Ads support Shopping campaigns?
Yes — Microsoft Shopping works similarly to Google Shopping. You need a Microsoft Merchant Center account and a product feed. You can import your Google Merchant Center feed directly.

Is Bing Ads worth it for a small budget?
Below $500/month total, probably not — volume will be too low. At $1,000+/month on Google, adding $150–$200 on Bing is almost always worth testing.
