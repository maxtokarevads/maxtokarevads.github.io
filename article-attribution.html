Cross-Platform Attribution: How to Know Which Ad Actually Drove the Sale

Max Tokarev 📅 January 2026 ⏱ 10 min read
Quick Answer

When running Google, Meta and TikTok simultaneously, platform-reported conversions total 2–3× your actual sales due to attribution overlap. Use GA4 with Data-Driven Attribution as your source of truth. Platform dashboards are directional only. Budget decisions should be based on GA4 numbers, not what each platform claims.

Here's a scenario I see constantly: a client runs Google Ads, Meta Ads and TikTok simultaneously. Google Ads reports 180 conversions. Meta reports 240. TikTok reports 60. The actual number of orders in Shopify? 210. Every platform is double- and triple-counting.

Attribution is broken by design — each platform takes credit. I set up proper attribution as part of every campaign I manage for every conversion it touched. The fix is building your own source of truth outside the platforms.

Why Platform Attribution Lies

Every ad platform uses its own attribution window and model. Meta defaults to a 7-day click + 1-day view window. Google defaults to data-driven attribution. TikTok uses a 7-day click window by default. When a customer sees a TikTok ad on Monday, clicks a Google Search ad on Wednesday, then converts on Friday — all three platforms claim the conversion.

The only source of truth: GA4 with proper UTM parameters + your CRM or order management system. Platform dashboards are directional, not definitive.

Step 1: Build a Proper UTM Structure

UTM parameters are tags you add to your ad URLs that GA4 reads. Without them, GA4 often misattributes paid traffic as "Direct" or "Organic."

My standard UTM structure for each platform:

  • Google Search: utm_source=google&utm_medium=cpc&utm_campaign=CAMPAIGN_NAME&utm_content=AD_GROUP
  • Meta: utm_source=meta&utm_medium=paid_social&utm_campaign={{campaign.name}}&utm_content={{adset.name}}
  • TikTok: utm_source=tiktok&utm_medium=paid_social&utm_campaign=__CAMPAIGN_NAME__&utm_content=__ADGROUP_NAME__
  • LinkedIn: utm_source=linkedin&utm_medium=paid_social&utm_campaign=CAMPAIGN

Use each platform's dynamic parameters so names auto-populate. Never use spaces in UTM values — use hyphens or underscores.

Step 2: Set Up GA4 Data-Driven Attribution

GA4's default attribution model is "Last click" which gives all credit to the final touchpoint. Switch to Data-driven attribution (DDA) — it uses ML to distribute credit across all touchpoints based on their actual contribution to conversion.

To change it: GA4 → Admin → Attribution Settings → Reporting attribution model → Data-driven. Google's official GA4 attribution guide explains how DDA distributes credit across channels.

DDA requires at least 400 conversions per month across all channels to work accurately. Below that, use "Position-based" (40% first touch, 40% last touch, 20% middle).

Step 3: The Simple Reporting Dashboard

I use a Google Sheets dashboard that pulls data from three sources:

  1. GA4 (via Looker Studio connector) — sessions, conversions, revenue by source/medium
  2. Platform dashboards — spend per platform (manually updated weekly or via API)
  3. CRM/Shopify — actual orders and revenue (the ground truth)

The key column I track: GA4-attributed ROAS vs Platform-reported ROAS. When Meta claims 4.5× ROAS but GA4 shows 2.1× for the same period — that gap tells me Meta is over-attributing. I use GA4's number for budget decisions, not Meta's.

Step 4: Use Northbeam, Triple Whale or Hyros (Optional)

For e-commerce doing $50K+/month in revenue, a dedicated attribution tool is worth it. These tools build a pixel-independent view using first-party data and probabilistic matching.

  • Triple Whale — best for Shopify e-commerce, easy setup
  • Northbeam — more advanced, good for multi-channel brands
  • Hyros — popular for info products and high-ticket offers

At $300–$500/month, these tools pay for themselves quickly if you're spending $10K+/month on ads and making budget decisions based on platform-reported ROAS.

The Practical Rule for Budget Decisions

When platforms disagree with GA4, I use this hierarchy:

  1. CRM/Shopify — total orders (most reliable)
  2. GA4 DDA — channel-level attribution
  3. Platform dashboards — directional only, for creative and audience decisions

Never kill a channel because its platform-reported ROAS looks bad. Check GA4 first. I've seen cases where Meta looked like a failure on its own dashboard but was the #2 channel by GA4's attribution — it was warming up audiences that converted on Google.

Frequently Asked Questions

Why does Meta always claim more conversions than actually happened?
Meta's view-through attribution (counting conversions where someone only saw an ad, didn't click) inflates numbers significantly. Turn off view-through attribution in your Meta settings if you want a cleaner picture. Go to Events Manager → Attribution settings → set view window to "None."

What's the simplest attribution setup for a small business?
UTM parameters on all ads + GA4 with Last Non-Direct Click model. Takes 2 hours to set up and gives you a reliable enough picture for $500–$3,000/month ad spend.

How do I track phone call conversions across platforms?
Use a call tracking tool like CallRail or WhatConverts. These assign unique phone numbers per traffic source and report back to GA4 and each ad platform. Essential for local businesses and legal/medical where calls are the primary conversion.
